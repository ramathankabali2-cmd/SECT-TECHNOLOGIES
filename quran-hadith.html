Qur'an & Hadith

Guidance for Protecting Allah's Creation

The Qur'an and the teachings of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ remind us that caring for the Earth is part of our duty as Allah's trustees.

Environmental Stewardship in Islam

Islam teaches that the Earth belongs to Allah and humanity has been entrusted with its care. Muslims are encouraged to preserve nature, avoid waste, show kindness to animals and protect the environment for future generations.

These teachings demonstrate that environmental conservation is not merely a social responsibility but also an act of worship.

Qur'anic Verses

Surah Al-A'raf (7:56)

وَلَا تُفْسِدُوا فِي الْأَرْضِ بَعْدَ إِصْلَاحِهَا وَادْعُوهُ خَوْفًا وَطَمَعًا ۚ إِنَّ رَحْمَتَ اللَّهِ قَرِيبٌ مِّنَ الْمُحْسِنِينَ

"And cause not corruption upon the earth after its reformation. And invoke Him in fear and aspiration. Indeed, the mercy of Allah is near to the doers of good."

Reflection: Allah commands believers to avoid spreading corruption. Pollution, deforestation, destruction of wildlife and environmental degradation contradict this guidance. Protecting nature is part of preserving the order that Allah established.

Surah Ar-Rum (30:41)

ظَهَرَ الْفَسَادُ فِي الْبَرِّ وَالْبَحْرِ بِمَا كَسَبَتْ أَيْدِي النَّاسِ لِيُذِيقَهُم بَعْضَ الَّذِي عَمِلُوا لَعَلَّهُمْ يَرْجِعُونَ

"Corruption has appeared throughout the land and sea by [reason of] what the hands of people have earned so He may let them taste part of [the consequence of] what they have done that perhaps they will return [to righteousness]."

Reflection: This verse reminds humanity that many environmental problems result from human actions. Islam encourages responsibility, repentance and corrective action rather than neglect.

Surah Al-An'am (6:141)

وَهُوَ الَّذِي أَنشَأَ جَنَّاتٍ مَّعْرُوشَاتٍ وَغَيْرَ مَعْرُوشَاتٍ وَالنَّخْلَ وَالزَّرْعَ مُخْتَلِفًا أُكُلُهُ وَالزَّيْتُونَ وَالرُّمَّانَ مُتَشَابِه...ِهِ ۖ وَلَا تُسْرِفُوا ۚ إِنَّهُ لَا يُحِبُّ الْمُسْرِفِينَ

"And He it is who produces gardens trellised and untrellised, and date palms, and crops of different shape and taste, and olives and pomegranates, similar and dissimilar. Eat of their fruit when they ripen, but pay their due on the day of harvest. And do not be excessive. Indeed, He does not like those who commit excess."

Reflection: Islam teaches moderation. Whether using food, water or natural resources, Muslims should avoid waste and use Allah's blessings responsibly.

Surah Al-Baqarah (2:205)

وَيُهْلِكَ الْحَرْثَ وَالنَّسْلَ ۗ وَاللَّهُ لَا يُحِبُّ الْفَسَادَ

"...and strives to destroy crops and livestock. Allah does not like corruption."

Reflection: Destroying crops, forests, wildlife or ecosystems is condemned. Muslims should instead work to preserve and improve the Earth.

Authentic Hadith on Caring for the Environment

🌳 Planting Trees is Charity

"No Muslim plants a tree or sows seeds, and then a bird, a person, or an animal eats from it, except that it is regarded as charity for him."

Source: Sahih al-Bukhari (2320) and Sahih Muslim (1553)

Lesson: Every effort to grow plants or trees benefits creation and earns reward from Allah.

🌍 Stewardship of the Earth

"The world is sweet and green, and Allah is going to install you as stewards over it, so He will see how you act."

Source: Sahih Muslim (2742)

Lesson: Human beings are caretakers, not owners. We are accountable for how we use the Earth's resources.

🌱 Continue Doing Good

"If the Hour (Day of Judgment) is established while one of you has a sapling in his hand, then if he can plant it before it is established, let him plant it."

Source: Musnad Ahmad (12902). Classified as authentic by a number of contemporary scholars.

Lesson: Muslims should continue doing beneficial deeds, including caring for the environment, even when circumstances seem difficult.

Lessons from the Qur'an & Hadith

Plant More Trees

Trees provide food, shade and cleaner air while earning continuous reward.

Use Water Wisely

Avoid wasting water in daily life, even when it is plentiful.

Reduce Waste

Reuse and recycle materials to reduce pollution.

Protect Living Creatures

Islam encourages kindness towards animals and all living beings.

Put Faith into Action

The teachings of the Qur'an and the Sunnah remind us that protecting the environment is a responsibility entrusted to every believer. Small actions performed consistently can make a great difference in preserving Allah's creation.

Plant a Tree

Plant at least one tree and help create a greener future.

Keep the Environment Clean

Dispose of waste responsibly and encourage others to do the same.

Conserve Water

Use water wisely and avoid unnecessary wastage.

Protect Nature

Respect wildlife, forests and every blessing Allah has provided.

Our Green Pledge
